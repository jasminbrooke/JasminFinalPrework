Birds of Long Island

Long Island, NY is an island off the coast of New York. This densely populated island stretches 118 miles from New York Harbor to Montauk Point over various neighborhoods. But did you know it is also densely populated by birds? Parks, salt marshes, estuaries, shorelines... all these different habitats make it a suitable home for an array of species. From suburban towns to beautiful beaches, opportunities to see birds of all shapes, sizes and colors are so abundant that you do not have to leave your local area! In fact, here are some common yet beautiful birds you may see, even in your own backyard.


Northern Cardinal

One of the most beloved birds in the eastern United States is the Northern Cardinal. It generally lives in forests, thickets and suburban gardens and is easy to attract to backyard feeders. Known for its bright crimson red plumage, the male of this species will feed the fawn (light brown) female as part of its courtship behavior. Their diet includes grains, seeds and insects. They love sunflower seeds!


Blue Jay

While just as recognizable as the Northern Cardinal, the sight of a Blue Jays at a feeder is a little more divisive. They can be considered noisy and aggressive, and may chase smaller songbirds away from their preferred food sources, which consists of seeds, nuts, insects and soft fruits. On the upside, they are also great at alerting others to the presence of hawks. The male and female mate for life, and are similar in size and plumage. They work together to build nests and care for young. These Corvids are extremely intelligent!


Downy Woodpecker

The smallest woodpecker in the United States, the Downy Woodpecker lives in most forests. They mainly eat insects by foraging in the bark of trees. The male and female appear similar, but the male has a red patch on the back of it's head. A common visitor to backyard bird feeders, attract them with specialized suet cakes.


Red-tailed Hawk

Adaptable and opporuntistic, The Red-Tailed Hawk can be seen throughout North America and Canada. They mostly eat small mammals, such as squirrels and rabbits, but will also eat other birds and reptiles. The females can be up to a quarter larger than the males. They can often be seen roosting on tall trees near highways.


Hooded Merganser

The Hooded Merganser is a type of water fowl that lives on lakes and ponds throughout North America. They mostly eat fish and hunt by sight by diving underwater. They display a high level of sexual dimorphism during the breeding season. The females are mostly brown with a reddish crest. The males have black and white plumage on their wings, neck and head and yellow eyes. Look for them at your local duck pond, along with Mallards and Wood Ducks. Remember, do not feed bread to the ducks!
